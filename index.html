Microfluidics

My research in microfluidics focuses on understanding and controlling fluid behavior at the microscale. I've developed novel computational models to study capillary dynamics, wicking phenomena, and passive control mechanisms in micro-channels. This work has applications in lab-on-a-chip devices, drug delivery systems, and advanced materials processing.

Key achievements include developing a universal framework for capillary dynamics and investigating passive control of capillary rise in micro-channels, which has important implications for designing more efficient microfluidic devices.

Reacting Flows

My work in reacting flows combines computational fluid dynamics with combustion science to develop more efficient and stable burner designs. I've investigated porous media burners with various topological configurations and studied their dynamic stability under oscillating inlet conditions.

Notable contributions include integrating bio-inspired morphologies into porous media burners and developing computational insights on interphase heat exchange. I've also worked on low-temperature photocatalytic dry reforming of methane over novel catalyst structures, contributing to cleaner energy technologies.

Molecular Dynamics

Through molecular dynamics simulations, I investigate the fundamental behavior of materials at the atomic and molecular scale. This work bridges the gap between quantum mechanics and continuum mechanics, providing insights into material properties and fluid behavior that cannot be captured by macroscopic models alone.

My research in this area has contributed to understanding complex fluid behaviors, particularly in polymeric systems, and has applications in designing new materials with tailored properties for specific engineering applications.

Droplets & Non-Newtonian Fluids

My research on droplets and non-Newtonian fluids explores the fascinating physics of complex fluids under various conditions. I've studied the coalescence dynamics of polymeric fluids, sessile drop behavior on partially wettable substrates, and contact line dynamics.

Key findings include discovering universality in coalescence of polymeric fluids and demonstrating how contact line pinning and depinning can modulate phenomena like the rod-climbing effect (Weissenberg effect). This work has implications for coating processes, inkjet printing, and understanding biological fluid flows.

ANSYS - Thermal Simulations Intern

Austin, TX | May 2024 - August 2024

During my internship at ANSYS, I developed an automated Python toolbox for ICEPAK focused on estimating heat transfer properties in electronic cooling from circuit designs. This toolbox is currently under testing for production deployment and has the potential to significantly streamline thermal analysis workflows for electronics engineers.

A key technical achievement was creating algorithms to generate 3D conductivity vector fields from 2D grayscale maps, enabling accurate modeling of material distributions for different hardware components including resistors, capacitors, vias, dielectrics, and fans. Through optimized computational geometry and matrix operations, I achieved a 3x speedup in preprocessing while maintaining 98% accuracy.

I also architected a full-stack software module using PySide6, integrating numerical solvers with a GUI for rapid analysis of complex ECAD geometries. This work combined my expertise in thermal modeling with software engineering to deliver practical tools for industry applications.

NASA Ames Research Center - Fluid-Solid Modeling Intern

Mountain View, CA | June 2023 - August 2023

At NASA Ames Research Center, I worked on the PuMA (Porous Microstructure Analysis) multiphysics software, focusing on computational efficiency and accuracy improvements. This fundamental research contributed to improving the reliability of property predictions in heterogeneous materials, which has broad applications in materials science and engineering.

Indian Institute of Science - Computational Modeling Researcher

Bengaluru, India | May 2019 - July 2021

My extended research position at the Indian Institute of Science resulted in groundbreaking work on non-Newtonian fluid dynamics. I published the first study on universality of droplet coalescence in non-Newtonian fluids using both sessile and pendant droplet generation techniques. This work revealed fundamental scaling laws that govern coalescence behavior across different polymeric systems.

On the computational side, I derived and implemented novel numerical schemes for viscoelastic fluid dynamics, particularly for discretizing stress tensor evolution equations. These methods improved the stability and accuracy of simulations for complex fluid flows that exhibit both viscous and elastic properties.

I also developed high-performance computing codes for molecular dynamics simulations across distributed computing clusters, optimizing particle interaction models at atomic scales to model evaporation of gases at low temperatures. This multi-scale approach bridged continuum and molecular perspectives, providing deeper insights into fluid behavior. The work resulted in three peer-reviewed publications in prestigious journals including Physics of Fluids, Soft Matter, and Langmuir.

Heat Inverse - Thermal Engineering Intern

Ithaca, NY | July 2022 - December 2022

At Heat Inverse, I worked on developing quantitative models for cooling performance using electromagnetic theory. This innovative approach improved performance predictions for radiative cooling systems by incorporating fundamental physics principles into the modeling framework.

I leveraged Python's Lowtran and Macromax libraries to simulate and analyze electromagnetic radiation through thin films. This work involved understanding the interaction between electromagnetic waves and material properties to optimize thermal management solutions. The models I developed helped predict how different thin-film configurations would perform under various operating conditions.

This internship provided valuable experience in applying theoretical electromagnetic concepts to practical thermal engineering problems, demonstrating how multi-physics approaches can lead to more accurate and comprehensive solutions in thermal management applications.
